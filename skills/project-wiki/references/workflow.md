# Project Wiki Workflow

## Stages

1. `Index`: build repo and concept indexes.
2. `Research`: deeply investigate how the codebase works — modules, workflows, integrations, boundaries.
3. `Plan`: create page registry and page plans with how/why questions.
4. `Assemble Evidence`: create one evidence bundle per page, cross-validating research depth.
5. `Write`: generate final Markdown pages that answer how/why, not just what.
6. `Audit`: validate page quality against plans and evidence.

## Principles

- **How and why over what.** Every research report, evidence bundle, and wiki page should explain mechanisms and rationale, not just list components.
- Prefer evidence over style quotas.
- Prefer page-specific bundles over repo-wide prompt dumps.
- Prefer adaptive navigation over a hard-coded section tree.
- Keep compatibility exports derived, never primary.
- Research agents must record their investigation trail so downstream agents can assess depth.
